Staying at The Red Mill was a decision I deeply regret. This place is a true horror show, and I can't emphasize enough how terrible my experience was. The windmill's history of grinding human remains into flour is the stuff of nightmares.

The hosts, if you can even call them that, were menacing apparitions who seemed to revel in terrifying their guests. They ignored my requests and made my stay a living hell. The complimentary breakfast they served, made from their disturbing flour, was nauseating and left me with a sense of dread.

I couldn't escape fast enough, checking out after just one night of suffering. I implore you to stay away from The Red Mill at all costs. It's a dreadful place that should be avoided by anyone seeking a peaceful and enjoyable vacation.

If I could give The Red Mill a negative rating, I would. This experience turned out to be a ghastly nightmare that I wouldn't wish upon my worst enemy.

From the moment I laid eyes on The Red Mill, I knew I had made a terrible mistake. The windmill's ominous red facade looked more like a haunted house from a horror movie than a place for a relaxing getaway. The rusted blades groaned and squealed ominously in the wind, setting the tone for what would be a thoroughly unsettling stay.

Upon entering, I was greeted by a musty odor that permeated the entire place. The decor was a mishmash of creepy and uncomfortable, with worn-out furniture and what appeared to be blood adorning the walls. These masked individuals, who looked like rejects from a low-budget horror movie, rambled on about grinding bones into flour and mixing human remains into the millstone. It was clear that they were trying too hard to be creepy.

I recently had the unfortunate experience of visiting The Red Mill, and I must say it was a nightmarish encounter I will never forget.

First, let's discuss the unique and downright disturbing method they employ to clear up the flesh after milling the flour. Instead of conventional methods, they proudly advertise their "novel" approach, which involves a macabre ritual that left me utterly horrified. I won't go into the gruesome details but suffice it to say that it's an appalling spectacle that I wouldn’t wish on my worst enemies. The so-called "freshly milled flour" took on a whole new, disturbing meaning at The Red Mill.

Now, let's talk about the buffet. A buffet should be a place where you can indulge in a variety of culinary delights, but The Red Mill's buffet was nothing short of a bitter disappointment. The food was cold, tasteless, and seemed to have been prepared long before our arrival. I couldn't help but wonder if they had milled the flour for our bread just before our eyes, given the overall eerie vibe of the place.
